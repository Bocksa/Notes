Semi Permeable Membranes

Chapter 13 - Diffusion and Osmosis / Semi Permeable Membranes

A selectively/semi permeable membrane allows some but not all substances to pass through

  • All biological membranes are similar, this means that the cell membrane is similar to the membranes around mitochondria,
    chloroplasts or the nucleus
  • All these membranes are selectively permeable. This means that some molecules can pass easily through the membrane
    while other molecules cannot easily cross the membrane
  • Water, Oxygen and Carbon Dioxide can pass easily across membranes
  • Salts, starch, sugars and proteins are molecules that cannot easily pass across membranes
