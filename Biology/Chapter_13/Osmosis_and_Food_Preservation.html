Osmosis and Food Preservation

Chapter 13 - Diffusion and Osmosis / Osmosis and Food Preservation

Bacteria and fungi both have cell walls. If they are placed in a very concentrated solution they will lose water by osmosis. This
prevents them from growing and causing food to decay. There are two main examples of this process.

  1. Soaking Food in very salty solutions
    • Preserves fish and meat such as bacon
  2. High Sugar Content
    • Such as jam and marmalade. Preserves them from fungus decay
