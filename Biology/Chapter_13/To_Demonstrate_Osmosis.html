To Demonstrate Osmosis

Chapter 13 - Diffusion and Osmosis / To Demonstrate Osmosis

  1. Soak length of visking tubing in water (visking tubing is a semi-permeable membrane and soaking it makes it soft and
    pliable)
  2. Tie a knot in one end of visking tubing
  3. Fill with starch solution whose molecules are too big to pass through the tubing
  4. Seal the other end of visking tubing
  5. Dry the tube and weigh it. Note how full it is
  6. Place the tubing in a beaker of distilled water
  7. Repeat steps 1-5 except fill the visking tubing with distilled water to act as control
  8. Leave apparatus for about 30 mins
  9. Remove the tubes from the water, dry them and weight hem

Results: Tubing with starch solution expands and becomes more full. It gains weight - this happens because water moves due
to osmosis from a high water concentration to a low water concentration in the visking tubing
