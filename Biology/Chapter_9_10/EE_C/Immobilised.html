Bioprocessing with Immobilised Enzymes

Enzymes - Experiments / Bioprocessing with Immobilised Enzymes

Bioprocessing: use of living organisms or their products to produce useful substances

The organisms or the enzyme are frequently Immobilised

  • Immobilised are fixed to each other or trapped in a gel
The reaction takes place in a bioreactor
  • Bioreactor: A Vessel in which products are made by cells or organisms

Immobilising the Enzyme

  1. Mix enzyme (yeast) with sodium alginate solution
  2. Using a syringe drip this into calcium chloride solution
  3. Beads form
  4. Leave to harden for 10 minutes
  5. Filter off and wash

Using the Immobilized Enzyme

  1. Place sucrose solution in a beaker
  2. Add beads of immobilized yeast (enzyme)
  3. Leave for 10 minutes stirring occasionally
  4. Test for glucose using a clinistix strip

Result: Turns purple showing glucose present
