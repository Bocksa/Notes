Lacteal

Small Intestine / Lacteal

  • Each lacteal contains a liquid called lymph
  • Fatty acids and cells are absorbed into the cells of the villus lining
  • They reform into fats
  • The fats are coated in protein
  • They pass into the lymph in the lacteal
  • The fats are transported by the lymph
  • The fats get carried to the bloodstream in lymph vessels
  • The Protein coat dissolves
  • Blood and fats are absorbed into the cells
